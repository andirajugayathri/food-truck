What is Commercial Food Truck Insurance?

Commercial Food Truck Insurance is a specialized business insurance policy tailored for mobile food vendors, coffee vans, ice cream trucks, and catering vehicles. It combines vehicle insurance with business liability and asset protection, covering risks that are specific to operating a food business on wheels.

Coverage (Inclusions & Exclusions)

What’s Usually Included

Depending on your policy, coverage may include:

  • Public Liability Insurance
  • Commercial Vehicle Insurance
  • Product Liability Insurance
  • Equipment & Contents Cover
  • Fire & Explosion Cover
  • Business Interruption
  • Theft & Malicious Damage

Common Exclusions

Depending on your policy, coverage may include:

  • Intentional or illegal acts
  • Poor hygiene / Council failure
  • Normal wear and tear
  • Mechanical breakdown
  • Operating without licenses

⚠️ Always check the Product Disclosure Statement (PDS) for exact inclusions.

Market Insights

Interesting Things Food Truck Owners Often Overlook

  • Stricter council and event requirements:

    Many Australian councils and major event organizers now require higher public liability limits (often $10–$20 million) before approving food truck permits.

  • Higher insurance premiums due to inflation:

    In 2025–2026, repair costs, legal fees, and replacement equipment prices have increased, leading to higher premiums across most food truck policies.

  • Food safety enforcement is tightening:

    State health authorities are increasing inspections, and insurers may decline claims if food safety records, temperature logs, or hygiene standards are not maintained.

  • Vehicle insurance complexity:

    Food trucks are custom-built, and many standard vehicle policies don't fully cover fit-outs, generators, or cooking equipment unless specifically listed.

  • Event-based and short-term insurance demand is growing:

    Pop-up events and seasonal festivals are driving demand for temporary food truck insurance, especially among part-time operators.
